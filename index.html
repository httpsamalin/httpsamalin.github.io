My Experiences

Gamelan Musician

In high school, I was a member of the school's Gamelan team. I had performed Gamelan musics such as Perang Besar, Togok, and Timang Burung. The performances were held in various places in Negeri Sembilan, upon invitation from the organizer to their prestigious events such as graduation, convocation, formal events and award ceremonies. Fun fact, my elder sister was also in the same team as I was and we had performed together numerous times.

Creative Writing

Words resonate my emotions better thus I spend most of my leisure time reading away novels, short essays and penning poems for fun. It began early during my childhood as I had phonetic issue and my parents helped me improve by reading and writing alot. My interest in literature increased and I started to freestyling short essays, pantuns, and the one I am doing most nowadays is poems! I also won essay-writing competition in high school and was awarded with Pencapaian Bahasa Melayu Penulisan Terbaik in elementary school.

Vice President

In my second year of Diploma, I was elected by votes as the Vice President of Information Management Community (IMCo). It was incredibly eye-opening experience and definitely helped me grow as a student as well as a person. Although it was undeniably one of the tough and challenging episodes of my life, at the very end, it was all worth it. I have learned alot from being a Vice President and it was more than just the title.

Youth Virtual Summit

This was also one of my experiences that I am proud to be a part of. This summit was a collaboration between my former association, IMCo and the students' representatives board at that time. It was such an honour to be one of the members of Board Of Director of the event, which were organized in a global scale. I was the Vice Secretary and worked closely with the MPPs, and my respected team members. This experience truly challenged my knowledge and insights. But, it also taught me valuable life lessons.

Jawatankuasa Perwakilan Kolej

Prior to my election of vice president, I was Biro Kerohanian for Jawantankuasa Perwakilan Kolej in my campus. I joined it in my first semester and it lasted for three semester. Juggling between the two was beyond my capabilities, thus it was wise to let one go. During my time as a JPK, I was still a newbie but thankfully with the help of my fellow team mates, I was able to carry out my responsibilites as much as I could. Being a JPK was definitely one of my favourite moments during my Diploma studies. This is because I got to know a lot of behind-the-scenes things and got close to highly important figures in campus.

Robotics

Despite shallow insights on robotics, I had the chance to compete in a robotics competition in University Sains Islam Malaysia, Nilai. I remember learning about Arduino, the prominent component used in developing a robotic product to be presented during the competition. Me and my group created an innovative walking stick for visually impaired persons using Arduino. The features of the product was able to sense danger, alert authorities when the sensor fail to sense movement from the user and build-in map navigation. Sadly, we did not win the competition. However, as a result from the competition, our passion for science and technology increased and able us to score well in our PT3.
